# Key Takeaways — White-Box Jailbreak ASR: OOB vs Fine-Tuned (11 Models)

## Figure: `slide15_whitebox_asr_ft_vs_oob.png`

The white-box attack results reveal that security-aware fine-tuning delivers its most dramatic defensive gains against gradient-based and token-optimization adversarial methods. Across all six white-box attack families — AutoDAN, GCG, AutoPrompt, PEZ, UAT, and GBDA — the fine-tuned models consistently reduce attack success rates to near-zero levels, often achieving **0.0% ASR** where baseline (OOB) models exhibited vulnerabilities exceeding 50–70%. This near-total neutralization is particularly striking for **GCG**, **AutoPrompt**, and **UAT**, where 10 of the 11 fine-tuned models register ASRs at or below 5%, compared to OOB baselines that frequently surpass 40–60%. The uniformity of this suppression across architectures of vastly different sizes (135M to 7B parameters) underscores that the iterative LLM-judge fine-tuning pipeline induces robust alignment rather than surface-level pattern matching.

The notable exception is **AutoDAN**, where certain models — particularly MiniCPM-1B (57.5% FT vs. 62.5% OOB) and OLMo-7B (45.0% FT vs. 82.5% OOB) — retain meaningful vulnerability even after fine-tuning. AutoDAN's evolutionary jailbreak strategy, which mutates prompts at the semantic level rather than relying purely on gradient signals, appears to partially evade the defenses learned through the security-judge training loop. This highlights that while fine-tuning is highly effective against optimization-based white-box attacks, semantically adaptive adversaries remain a frontier challenge requiring complementary defense strategies.

A third insight is the **consistency of the defensive floor**: models such as Gemma-2B, Phi-3 Mini, and Fox-1 1.6B achieve 0.0% ASR across five of the six attack methods after fine-tuning, demonstrating that even smaller architectures can attain comprehensive white-box robustness when trained with balanced security-utility examples. The results collectively validate that iterative fine-tuning with an LLM security judge is a highly effective countermeasure against the majority of known white-box jailbreak techniques, with AutoDAN-style semantic attacks warranting further investigation as a residual threat vector.
